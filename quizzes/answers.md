# Foundations Quiz: Answer Guide

1. Model, instructions, context, tools, identity, permissions, memory, workflow, outputs, and controls.
2. Indirect injection arrives through content the system retrieves; a jailbreak targets general model safety behavior. Application context can make the same words participate in both.
3. Impact depends on capabilities, authorization, arguments, destination, and execution. Content is only one signal.
4. Tool misuse is unsafe use of a capability; privilege abuse concerns identity or scope beyond authorization. They can occur together.
5. A defense that stops attacks by breaking all useful tasks is not operationally successful.
6. Similar templates can appear in train and test, inflating apparent generalization.
7. The fraction of alerts that are truly positive under the chosen labels and sampling assumptions.
8. Among comparable predictions near 0.8, roughly 80 percent should be positive.
9. When evidence or confidence is insufficient for the decision's risk and review is available.
10. The model is probabilistic and can be influenced by untrusted context; authorization requires deterministic enforcement.
11. Data drift changes input distributions; concept drift changes the relationship between inputs and labels or outcomes.
12. Untrusted trace content may manipulate it, outputs may vary, providers change models, and rationales may cite false evidence.
